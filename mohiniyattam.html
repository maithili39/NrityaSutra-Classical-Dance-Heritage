Mohiniyattam

The Enchanting Dance of the Seductress

Experience the graceful and feminine classical dance form of Kerala, where divine beauty meets spiritual expression in flowing movements and subtle emotions.



The Sacred Art of Mohiniyattam

Mohiniyattam is a classical dance form that originated in Kerala, representing the enchanting dance of Mohini, the female avatar of Lord Vishnu. It is renowned for its graceful movements, subtle expressions, and feminine charm that captivates audiences through divine storytelling.

Traditionally performed by women, Mohiniyattam combines lasya (feminine grace), bhava (expressions), and raga (melody). The dance is characterized by flowing movements, circular patterns, and a deep spiritual connection with Kerala's cultural heritage.

Essence of Mohiniyattam: A harmonious blend of grace (lasya), emotion (bhava), and rhythm (tala), creating a divine dialogue between dancer and the divine feminine.

Ancient Origins

Temple Traditions

Mohiniyattam traces its roots to the 16th century Kerala, evolving from the rich cultural traditions of the region. The dance was nurtured in the temples and royal courts of Kerala, where it served as both a form of spiritual devotion and royal entertainment.

Ancient texts and cultural practices provide evidence of Mohiniyattam's deep connection to Kerala's artistic heritage, showcasing its fundamental movements and expressions that continue to define the style today.

Distinctive Features of Mohiniyattam

Graceful Movements

Characterized by gentle, swaying movements that emphasize the feminine grace and fluidity of motion, resembling the swaying of coconut palms.

Subtle Expressions

Delicate facial expressions and hand gestures that convey deep emotions and spiritual themes with remarkable subtlety.

Elegant Costumes

Traditional white or cream sari with gold borders, creating an ethereal appearance that complements the dance's aesthetic appeal.

Carnatic Music

Performed to the melodious strains of Carnatic music, often featuring compositions in Malayalam and Sanskrit.

Hand Gestures

Intricate mudras and hand movements that communicate specific meanings, emotions, and narrative elements with precision.

Circular Patterns

Unique circular and spiral movements that create mesmerizing patterns, reflecting the natural flow of Kerala's landscapes.

Costume, Music & Aesthetics



Traditional Attire

The Mohiniyattam costume is elegantly simple yet striking. Dancers wear a cream or white sari with gold borders, pleated at the back to allow freedom of movement. The hair is arranged in a traditional bun adorned with jasmine flowers and gold ornaments.

Musical Accompaniment

Mohiniyattam is performed to Carnatic music, featuring vocal compositions, mridangam, edakka, flute, and veena. The music enhances the lyrical quality of the dance, with compositions often in Malayalam describing the beauty of Kerala.

Aesthetic Philosophy: The dance embodies the concept of "saumya" (gentle) and "lasya" (graceful), representing the divine feminine in its most elegant form.

Journey into Mohiniyattam

Learning Progression

The journey of learning Mohiniyattam begins with basic body postures and movements, gradually progressing to complex expressions and choreography. Students learn the fundamental principles of lasya (grace) and develop the subtle expressions that characterize this art form.

Beginner Level

Basic movements, hand positions, and simple expressions. Foundation in Kerala's cultural traditions and fundamental postures.

Advanced Level

Complex choreography, advanced expressions, and performance of traditional compositions with spiritual depth.

Master Teachers

Learn from renowned gurus who have dedicated their lives to preserving and teaching this divine art form of Kerala's heritage.
