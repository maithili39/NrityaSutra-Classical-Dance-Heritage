Bharatanatyam

The Timeless Dance of Tamil Nadu's Temples

Experience the geometric grace, expressive storytelling, and spiritual depth of Bharatanatyam, the oldest classical dance of India, rooted in the sacred temples of Tamil Nadu.



The Sacred Art of Bharatanatyam

Bharatanatyam is a classical dance form that originated in the temples of Tamil Nadu. It is renowned for its fixed upper torso, bent legs, intricate footwork, expressive gestures, and elaborate storytelling through dance and music.

Traditionally performed by Devadasis as an offering to the deities, Bharatanatyam combines nritta (pure dance), nritya (expressive dance), and natya (dramatic storytelling). The dance is characterized by geometric patterns, dynamic movements, and a deep spiritual connection.

Essence of Bharatanatyam: A harmonious blend of bhava (emotion), raga (melody), and tala (rhythm), creating a divine dialogue between dancer and audience.

Ancient Origins

Temple Traditions

Bharatanatyam traces its roots to the Natya Shastra, an ancient Sanskrit treatise on performing arts. The dance was nurtured in the temples of Tamil Nadu, especially in Chidambaram and Thanjavur, where Devadasis performed sacred rituals through dance.

Stone carvings in temples and ancient texts provide evidence of Bharatanatyam's antiquity, showcasing its fundamental postures and mudras that continue to define the style today.

Distinctive Features of Bharatanatyam

Aramandi Posture

The signature half-sitting posture, creating geometric lines and a strong foundation for movement.

Expressive Abhinaya

Masterful use of facial expressions and hand gestures (mudras) to convey stories and emotions.

Rhythmic Footwork

Complex patterns of stamping and ankle bells (ghungroos) synchronized with Carnatic music.

Mudras & Gestures

Codified hand gestures and body positions that narrate mythological tales and spiritual concepts.

Sculptural Beauty

Poses inspired by temple sculptures, bringing ancient art to life through dance.

Vivid Costumes

Vibrant silk costumes, ornate jewelry, and dramatic makeup enhance the visual splendor of performances.

Costume, Music & Aesthetics

Traditional Attire

Bharatanatyam dancers wear pleated silk sarees, temple jewelry, and elaborate headpieces. The costume accentuates every movement and pose.

  • Temple Jewelry: Gold-plated ornaments for head, ears, neck, arms, and waist
  • Ghungroos: Ankle bells that highlight rhythmic footwork
  • Makeup: Bold eyes and red lips for dramatic expression
Musical Accompaniment

Bharatanatyam is performed to Carnatic music, featuring:

  • Mridangam: Main percussion instrument
  • Violin: Melodic support
  • Flute: Adds lyrical beauty
  • Nattuvangam: Cymbals played by the dance teacher

Learning Path for Bharatanatyam

Begin your Bharatanatyam journey with a structured approach to mastering this sacred art.

Foundation Level

Basic adavus (steps), mudras, postures, and rhythm exercises to build strength and coordination.

Duration: 6-12 months
Age: 5+ years
Intermediate Level

Varnam, abhinaya, complex footwork, and repertoire pieces with deeper emotional expression.

Duration: 2-3 years
