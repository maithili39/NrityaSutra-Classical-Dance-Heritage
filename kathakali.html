Kathakali

The Epic Theater Dance of Kerala

Experience the spectacular dance-drama that combines mythology, martial arts, and magnificent costumes in theatrical splendor, bringing ancient Hindu epics to life.



The Sacred Theater of Kathakali

Kathakali is a classical dance-drama that originated in the 17th century in Kerala. It is renowned for its elaborate makeup, ornate costumes, powerful expressions, and dramatic storytelling that brings ancient Hindu epics to life through total theater experience.

Traditionally performed in temple courtyards and palace grounds, Kathakali combines dance (stylized movements), drama (character portrayal), music (vocal and instrumental), and martial arts (Kalaripayattu influence). The art form is characterized by larger-than-life characters, vigorous movements, and deep spiritual narratives.

Essence of Kathakali: A divine synthesis of vesham (costume and makeup), abhinaya (expression), and tala (rhythm), creating an immersive journey into the world of gods, demons, and heroes.

Ancient Origins

Royal Patronage

Kathakali evolved in the 17th century under the patronage of the Zamorin rulers of Calicut and other Kerala kingdoms. It developed from earlier theatrical forms like Krishnanattam and Ramanattam, gradually incorporating elements from martial arts like Kalaripayattu, giving it its distinctive vigorous and athletic quality.

The art form was traditionally performed in temple courtyards and palace grounds during festivals, serving both as entertainment and spiritual practice. The stories chosen were primarily from Hindu epics, making Kathakali a medium for transmitting cultural and religious values to the masses.

Distinctive Features of Kathakali

Elaborate Makeup

Spectacular face painting that transforms performers into divine and demonic characters, with different colors representing different character types.

Ornate Costumes

Magnificent, elaborate costumes with huge skirts, ornate headdresses, and intricate jewelry that create larger-than-life characters.

Powerful Expressions

Highly developed facial expressions and eye movements that convey complex emotions and narratives without spoken words.

Athletic Movements

Vigorous, martial arts-inspired movements that require exceptional physical strength, flexibility, and stamina.

Hand Gestures

Intricate mudras (hand gestures) that communicate specific meanings, emotions, and narrative elements to the audience.

Musical Drama

Integration of classical Carnatic music, percussion, and vocal narration that supports and enhances the dramatic performance.

Character Classifications

Kathakali characters are classified into distinct types, each with specific makeup, costume, and behavioral patterns.

Pacha (Green)

Noble heroes and virtuous characters like Rama, Krishna, and Arjuna. Green makeup symbolizes nobility and virtue.

Kathi (Knife)

Villainous characters with green base and red marks. Represents arrogant and evil kings like Ravana and Duryodhana.

Thaadi (Beard)

Divided into white, red, and black beard types representing different character traits from divine to demonic.

Minukku (Radiant)

Characters with gentle, refined nature like women, sages, and Brahmins. Simple, elegant makeup without elaborate decoration.

Costume, Makeup & Musical Ensemble




Transformative Artistry

The transformation of a performer into a Kathakali character is an art form in itself, taking several hours to complete:

  • Makeup Process: Takes 3-4 hours using natural pigments and rice paste
  • Kireedam: Elaborate headdresses specific to each character type
  • Costumes: Voluminous skirts, ornate jackets, and character-specific accessories
  • Chutti: White rice paste borders that frame the face dramatically
  • Eye Enhancement: Special techniques to make eyes appear larger and more expressive
Musical Accompaniment

Kathakali performances are supported by a traditional orchestra that creates the dramatic atmosphere:

  • Chenda: Primary percussion instrument providing rhythm
  • Maddalam: Barrel-shaped drum for accompaniment
  • Chengila: Gong that marks important moments
  • Elathalam: Small cymbals for delicate rhythms
  • Vocals: Singers who narrate the story and provide dialogue

Journey into Kathakali

Learning Kathakali is an intensive journey that demands physical strength, artistic sensitivity, and deep cultural understanding.

Physical Training

Kalaripayattu basics, flexibility exercises, and building stamina for demanding performances.

Character Study

Learning makeup techniques, costume handling, and embodying different character types.

Expression Mastery

Developing facial expressions, eye movements, and emotional depth for storytelling.

Performance Readiness

Complete story presentations, stage presence, and interaction with musical ensemble.
