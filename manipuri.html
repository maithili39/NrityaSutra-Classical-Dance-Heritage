Manipuri

The Divine Dance of Manipur

Experience the ethereal grace, devotional storytelling, and spiritual beauty of Manipuri, the classical dance form of Manipur, renowned for its Raas Leela and gentle, flowing movements.



The Sacred Art of Manipuri

Manipuri is a classical dance form from the northeastern state of Manipur. It is celebrated for its soft, lyrical movements, devotional themes, and the iconic Raas Leela performances depicting the love of Radha and Krishna.

Rooted in Vaishnavism, Manipuri dance is performed with gentle, rounded movements, avoiding sharp lines and emphasizing spiritual expression. The dance is traditionally accompanied by Manipuri classical music and unique percussion instruments.

Essence of Manipuri: A harmonious blend of devotion, grace, and storytelling, evoking the divine love and spiritual ecstasy of Krishna and Radha.

Sacred Origins

Temple & Ritual Traditions

Manipuri dance has its roots in the ancient rituals and festivals of Manipur, especially the Lai Haraoba festival, which celebrates the creation myths of the Meitei people. The dance evolved further with the influence of Vaishnavism, leading to the development of the Raas Leela tradition.

Manipuri was nurtured in temples and royal courts, with both men and women performing devotional dances as offerings to the deities.

Distinctive Features of Manipuri

Gentle, Flowing Movements

Movements are soft, rounded, and continuous, symbolizing the gentle nature of the Manipuri landscape.

Raas Leela

The iconic dance-drama depicting the divine love of Radha and Krishna, performed with devotion and grace.

Unique Music & Percussion

Accompanied by Manipuri classical music, pung (drum), kartal (cymbals), and flute.

Costume & Attire

Female dancers wear the unique Kumil (cylindrical skirt), translucent veil, and traditional jewelry.

Group Choreography

Often performed in groups, especially in Raas Leela, with synchronized, harmonious movements.

Spiritual Expression

Focuses on devotion, humility, and spiritual elevation through dance.

Costume, Music & Aesthetics

Traditional Attire

  • Kumil: Decorated cylindrical skirt for female dancers
  • Veil: Translucent white veil covering the head
  • Jewelry: Traditional Manipuri ornaments
Musical Accompaniment

Manipuri dance is performed to classical Manipuri music, featuring:

  • Pung: Main percussion drum
  • Kartal: Cymbals
  • Bansuri: Flute
  • Vocals: Devotional songs in Manipuri and Sanskrit

Learning Path for Manipuri

Begin your Manipuri journey with a focus on devotion, grace, and group harmony.

Foundation Level

Basic steps, hand gestures, gentle movements, and understanding Manipuri rhythm patterns.

Advanced Level

Raas Leela performances, group choreography, devotional expression, and spiritual abhinaya.

Learning Tips:
  • Practice gentle, rounded movements daily
  • Study Manipuri music and rhythm
  • Observe group coordination and harmony
  • Learn the devotional stories behind each piece
  • Embrace humility and spiritual focus in dance
